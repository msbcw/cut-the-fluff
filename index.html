The Three Levers

1. Moderate Caloric Deficit

Your TDEE is ~2,300 kcal. Eating ~1,850/day creates a 400-500 kcal deficit — enough to lose 0.5kg/week without misery. The key lever is protein: aim for 130-150g/day. This preserves muscle and keeps you full. Everything else (carbs, fats) is flexible around that protein target.

2. Resistance Training

Your current routine is great for general fitness but doesn't build muscle or reshape your body. We're adding 3-4 days of progressive resistance training. Muscle is what makes you look "lean" instead of just "thin" — without it, losing fat just makes you a smaller version of the same shape.

3. Consistency Over Perfection

You travel ~25-30% of your time. If the plan collapses during travel, you lose a third of your progress. So we build two modes: "home mode" (optimised) and "travel mode" (simplified but still effective). A B+ effort sustained beats an A+ effort that collapses every few weeks.

Training Plan

4-5 sessions per week, 30-40 minutes each. Tap any day to see the full workout with exercise details.

Phase 1 uses your apartment gym dumbbells, bodyweight, pool, and running track. The goal is to build the habit, learn the movements, and get strong enough to make a real gym worthwhile. After 4-6 weeks of consistent training, you'll be ready (and motivated) to transition.

Phase 2 assumes you've joined a gym with barbells, a squat rack, cables, and machines. The same push/pull/legs/circuit structure continues — you're not learning a new routine, just upgrading your tools. Barbells let you load progressively heavier, which is the primary driver of getting stronger and changing how your body looks.

Progression Rules

When you can complete all sets and reps with clean form, increase the weight next session — even by just 0.5-1kg. If you can't increase weight, add 1-2 reps per set. Write down your numbers after every session (use the Log tab). This simple habit is what separates people who get results from people who just "work out."

What to Cut or Reduce

You don't need to eliminate anything, but these are the highest-impact reductions: excessive rice portions (the single biggest calorie source in most SL diets), sweetened tea or Milo, fried short eats (rolls, cutlets, patties — calorie bombs), coconut milk-heavy gravies (use in moderation), and sugary biscuits with tea.

Travel Mode

Your trips are 1-2 weeks every 6-8 weeks. The goal during travel is simple: don't go backwards. You won't optimise — you just hold the line.

Morning Rule

Work out in the morning before the day starts. You already know this about yourself — evenings disappear to dinners, drinks, and colleagues. Set your alarm 35 minutes earlier. That's it. Non-negotiable on travel days.

Hotel Room Workout (20-25 min)

When there's no gym available, do 4 rounds of this circuit with 60-90 seconds rest between rounds:

→15 push-ups
→15 bodyweight squats
→10 lunges each leg
→10 burpees
→30-second plank

If the hotel has a gym with dumbbells, do your normal push or pull day with whatever's available. Something always beats nothing.

Minimum Viable Commitment

3 sessions per travel week. That's it. Three. If you hit three, you're winning. If you hit four, you're a hero. Don't aim for five and end up doing zero because the target felt impossible.

Eating in Germany & Europe

Breakfast (hotel buffet) — your easiest win

German hotel breakfasts are protein-rich if you choose well: load up on eggs (Rührei, gekochte Eier), cheese, Aufschnitt, yogurt, tomatoes. Skip the Brötchen basket and the Nutella. You know the drill — you grew up with this. Just choose the protein side of the buffet.

Lunch

Salad with protein from any restaurant, or grab a Hähnchenbrust-Salat from Rewe or Edeka for a few euros. German supermarkets also have great Magerquark (500g, ~60g protein, under €1) — if you can stomach it plain or with some berries, it's the best protein-per-euro deal in Europe.

Dinner (the social one)

This is where you'll drink and eat with colleagues or friends. That's fine — it's part of the trip. Choose grilled fish or meat with vegetables as your main. Skip the bread basket and the Kartoffelbeilage if you've had enough carbs. Eat slowly, enjoy the company, and don't stress about it being perfect.

The Alcohol Rule

Allow yourself 2-3 drinks in a social evening. Choose lower-calorie options where it doesn't feel forced (a Helles over a Weizen, wine or G&T over cocktails). But the real damage from alcohol isn't the calories in the drink — it's the Döner at midnight and the skipped morning workout. Avoid both. If you drink, set your alarm anyway and show up to the hotel gym even if it's only for 20 minutes. The habit matters more than the performance.

The Math That Matters

If you're in Sri Lanka 5-6 weeks and travel 1-2 weeks in an 8-week cycle, and you lose 0.5kg/week at home and just hold steady during travel, you still lose 2.5-3kg every two months. That's 10+ kg in 7-8 months. The people who fail aren't the ones with imperfect travel weeks — they're the ones who use travel as an excuse to abandon everything, gain 2kg, and spend two weeks getting back to where they started.
